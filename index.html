Monday · 9 March 2026

From Bruges to Friesland

A long train ride north — and a foggy first night in a city we'd never heard of.

📷 images/day1-train.jpg
Train from Bruges
📷 images/day1-city.jpg
Leeuwarden at night

Our journey started at Bruges station. A train across the flat, windmill-dotted Dutch landscape brought us to Leeuwarden by early evening — the capital of Friesland and, as we quickly discovered, a city full of quiet surprises.

"Small canals, old town charm, and a thick foggy atmosphere on arrival night. It felt like stepping into a painting."

We checked into our accommodation on Nieuwestad, took a short walk around the city centre, and called it an early night. Tomorrow, the real programme would begin.

Wednesday · 11 March 2026

AI, Teams & the City

AI Experience Centre, a team exercise, and a street art tour through Leeuwarden.

📷 images/day3-ai.jpg
AI Experience Centre
📷 images/day3-transform.jpg
Live AI camera demo

The most varied day of the week. We started at the AI Experience Centre — NHL Stenden's research lab for Computer Vision & AI. Live demos showed real-time face detection, a camera that transforms you into historical figures, defect detection systems, and plastic & insect classification.

📷 images/day3-team.jpg
"Worst Team" workshop
📷 images/day3-tour.jpg
Oldehove & street art tour

"We were asked to design the worst possible team — then fix it. Simple idea, surprisingly deep conversation."

The afternoon's International Street Art Tour took us past the leaning Oldehove tower, tiny toy figures hidden in city walls, pixelated Rembrandt murals, a ceramics museum, and a hotel lined with Dutch royal family portraits. Leeuwarden turned out to be an open-air gallery nobody told us about.

Thursday · 12 March 2026

Cybersecurity & Berlin

The Swiss Cheese Model, the Odido hack, and a live collaboration with students in Berlin.

📷 images/day4-workshop.jpg
Cybersafety workshop
📷 images/day4-berlin.jpg
Online session with HWR Berlin

Thursday was the most intensive day. Eric de Bruin and Maarten te Kulve led a morning workshop on cybersafety using the Swiss Cheese Model — a framework showing how security incidents happen when multiple protection layers fail at the same time.

"We applied it to the Odido hack — 0.5 million accounts exposed through a chain of small failures. Abstract theory became very real, very fast."

🧀
Cybersafety Workshop — Eric de Bruin & Maarten te Kulve
Swiss Cheese Model applied to the real Odido hack — understanding layered security failures.
🌍
Online Session — HWR Berlin × NHL Stenden · AIDeRS Project
Cross-border collaboration on a disaster management concept using AI for detection, protection, recovery and evacuation.

Dinner in the city centre that evening — relaxed, no schedule. After four packed days, it felt good to simply walk around Leeuwarden one more time and let the week sink in.

Friday · 13 March 2026

The Yellow Train Home

Checkout, a last walk through the city, and the train back to Bruges.

📷 images/day5-station.jpg
Leeuwarden station
📷 images/day5-train.jpg
NS yellow train · heading south

Friday was bittersweet. After checking out, we took a slow last walk through the city. The Leeuwarden train station — with its beautiful stained-glass windows and grand clock — made for a perfect final memory before boarding the yellow NS train south.

"Five days that started with a map search and ended with a city we genuinely didn't want to leave. Would we go back? Without question."

The ride back to Bruges gave us time to process everything: the lectures, the people, the ideas, and a city that quietly surprised us at every turn.
